Paper Submission Guidelines

These guidelines apply to working notes papers submitted by teams invited to publish in the IEEE BigData 2026 conference proceedings following the SRAM challenge.

Invitation Criteria

Teams will be evaluated and invited based on the following criteria:

  • Model performance (30%): Final composite score on the held-out test set.
  • Approach innovation (40%): Novelty of methods, use of clinical knowledge, or new architectures.
  • Report quality (30%): Clarity, completeness, and reproducibility of the submitted working notes.

Approximately the top 10 teams will be invited. Additional slots may be extended based on scientific merit.

Paper Format

  • Length: Up to 10 pages (including references), double-column IEEE format.
  • Template: Use the official IEEE conference template. Download from IEEE Templates.
  • Language: English only.
  • Anonymization: Papers are not anonymized — include all author names and affiliations.

Required Sections

Working notes papers should include:

  • System Overview: A brief description of your overall approach.
  • Method: Detailed description of your model(s), including architecture, training details, and any external resources used.
  • Results: Performance on both subtasks (if applicable), with ablation studies where possible.
  • Discussion: Error analysis and insights on model behavior with respect to clinical risk signals.
  • Conclusion: Summary and future directions.
  • Ethical Statement: A brief statement confirming compliance with the Data Usage Agreement and ethical use of the dataset.

Submission System

Paper submission will be handled through the IEEE BigData 2026 submission portal. The link will be provided to invited teams by email.
Current pointer: https://bigdataieee.org/BigData2026/ (updated when the portal opens).

Key Dates

  • Paper submission deadline: November 15, 2026
  • Notification of acceptance: November 25, 2026
  • Camera-ready deadline: December 1, 2026
  • Conference: December 14–17, 2026, Phoenix, Arizona, USA
